There is no shortage of book lists, but most of them are lists of books that *used to* sell. This study asks a different question: **which evergreen books are people still actively posting about in 2026?**

The method is straightforward — one research sweep per category across Threads, Dcard, PTT, Vocus, Medium, and Substack, admitting only books with fresh posts, reviews, or debates between the second half of 2025 and mid-2026. Every book comes with a concrete community-evidence link. The bar is "still being discussed," not "still on sale."

## The Biggest Finding First: The Rewire Phenomenon

The strongest single signal in Taiwan's H1 2026 book market is [Nicole Vignola's *Rewire*](https://www.cw.com.tw/article/5141940): **third place on both** the Eslite and Books.com.tw half-year bestseller charts, briefly out of stock, and a CommonWealth Magazine author interview on 2026-07-08 headlined "Why did a neuroscience book blow up in Taiwan?" The breakout started with a Threads post in August 2025 that drew nearly 5,000 likes, and the fire kept spreading — [a podcast-highlights thread in May 2026](https://www.threads.com/@billy4select/post/DYOlwOpmTKC/), a 957-like passage roundup in late June, and fresh reader posts into early July.

The phenomenon matters on two levels: "neuroplasticity" crossed over from academic jargon to mainstream vocabulary, and it demonstrated how Threads can push a book from niche to top-3 on both major charts — an observation this post returns to at the end.

## Productivity / Execution

The shared engine across this category in 2026 is "productivity in the AI era": efficiency books are being reframed as "how to harness AI while protecting your focus."

- ***Atomic Habits* (James Clear)** — the anchor of the Chinese-language productivity shelf, with the translation past its 190th printing. In 2026 readers are still [on their third read (2023→2025→2026)](https://www.threads.com/@zane_junn/post/DY_tgAjjwn1/), with [new recommendation posts in June](https://www.threads.com/@zhustudio/post/DVVvgi4E7fU/); the *Atomic Habits Workbook*, released in March, sparked a wave of hands-on discussion, and the "overrated / just common sense" backlash keeps the conversation alive.
- ***The 12 Week Year* (Brian Moran)** — the "compress a year into a 12-week sprint, drop the annual plan" execution framework, beloved by Notion and AI-tool users. Representative discussions: freeshiuan's [reading reflection on Threads](https://www.threads.com/@freeshiuan/post/DHqbMZvSJ1W/) and the guide [Building a 12-week plan with Notion + ChatGPT](https://freeshiuan.com/the-12-week-year/) (in Chinese) — wiring the methodology directly into AI workflows.
- ***Deep Work* (Cal Newport)** — the book whose discussion *grew* after the AI explosion. The community framing: "[master deep work and you can drive AI instead of being driven by it](https://jamread.com/deep-work/)" (in Chinese) — focus redefined as the moat that can't be automated.
- ***The Productivity Project* (Chris Bailey)** — the author ran a year of self-experiments testing 25 productivity techniques. Vocus [still published new reviews in 2025](https://vocus.cc/article/679a2485fd89780001dc1155) (in Chinese), angled at biological prime time and "accomplishment over busyness."
- ***Four Thousand Weeks* (Oliver Burkeman)** — the anti-productivity productivity book. [Reading Outpost](https://readingoutpost.com/4-thousand-weeks/) and [other review sites](https://bookandyoga.com/four-thousand-weeks/) (in Chinese) tie it to FOMO and time anxiety; it is routinely prescribed as the antidote after a stack of efficiency books.

## Life Design / Career

The dominant note in career communities at the start of 2026 is AI disruption plus record graduate unemployment — [BetweenGos cites CNBC and MIT data](https://betweengos.com/2026-ai-job-market-strength-based-mindset/) (in Chinese): 89% of HR leaders expect AI to hit jobs in 2026, and US new-graduate unemployment is near 10%. That anxiety pushed the whole "redesign your life" toolbox back onto the table.

- ***Designing Your Life* (Bill Burnett & Dave Evans)** — nearly a decade in print and still the default answer to career drift in the Chinese-speaking world. Threads discussion runs from [year-end 2025 book lists](https://www.threads.com/@melo.record/post/DS4uM83j6B_) through July 2026; most interestingly, it gets wired straight into AI job-hunting anxiety — [career-coach accounts in AI job-search threads recommend the book's methods for inventorying your strengths](https://www.threads.com/@peter_career_hack/post/DZiG6qRCNS3/) (the addition/subtraction exercises) rather than handing your résumé to AI. Even the contrarian Dcard thread "[this book looks like a scam](https://www.dcard.tw/f/book/p/259894830)" (in Chinese) draws engagement — adversarial discussion is itself evidence of life. The sequel *Designing Your Work Life* is usually recommended as a pair.
- ***Hidden Potential* (Adam Grant)** — a Books.com.tw top-100 pick of 2024. "Progress depends on how you learn and on character skills, not talent" overlaps heavily with the AI-era lifelong-learning narrative; [Vocus has a dedicated breakdown](https://vocus.cc/article/678f9568fd89780001add12e) (in Chinese), and Reading Outpost even wrote to the author and got a reply.
- ***How Will You Measure Your Life?* (Clayton Christensen)** — the legendary final Harvard Business School lecture, reliably pulled off the shelf at the "high salary but unhappy" crossroads. [Reading Outpost's Threads walkthrough](https://www.threads.com/@readingoutpost/post/DPINy23CqOi/) and a [Vocus career essay](https://vocus.cc/article/6a2e70b5fd8978000163be8c) (in Chinese) both show recent engagement.
- ***Work Without a Job* (Waki Chen) × *Company of One* (Paul Jarvis)** — the pair that matches 2026's strongest community mood: quitting without a backup plan, side projects, personal brands. The Threads post "[books that make you want to resign](https://www.threads.com/@pinki.ouo/post/DR3pd3FkooB/)" hit 34.2K views and 490 likes with these titles at #2 and #3; H1 2026 produced a whole genre of "N months after quitting" book-list posts.
- Runner-up: ***Grit* (Angela Duckworth)** — [career-coach accounts keep citing it](https://www.threads.com/@leo_lifecoach_/post/DNlcIXMS7bp/), but it is a standing classic rather than a 2026 talking point.

## Brain Science / Neuroplasticity

- ***Rewire* (Nicole Vignola)** — the 2026 breakout described above, positioned by the community as "the brain-science edition of *Atomic Habits*": hardware (the brain) versus software (thought habits). Discussion has matured into a pro/con debate — the translation quality and the marketing-heavy subtitle both draw criticism, which is usually the surest sign a book has genuinely arrived.
- ***Dopamine Nation* (Anna Lembke)** — the evergreen theoretical source for digital-detox and short-video-addiction discourse. Threads was still producing remixes of the "[30-day DOPAMINE reset](https://www.threads.com/@chengyuanchieh/post/DY-IxPCgcNU/)" in May–June 2026; the community's core line is "the opposite of addiction is not sobriety, it is connection."
- ***Why We Sleep* (Matthew Walker)** — the sleep-neuroscience bible, with steady walkthroughs on the [Dcard books board](https://www.dcard.tw/f/book/p/233493054) (in Chinese), doctors' Facebook pages, and Vocus, resonating with Taiwan's insomnia-generation context.
- ***The Brain That Changes Itself* (Norman Doidge)** — the founding classic of popular neuroplasticity. After *Rewire* blew up, [Threads users started pointing newcomers back to this "earlier, harder-core" source text](https://www.threads.com/@bbor0201/post/DXB63N-CYff/) — a classic getting second exposure on a new book's coattails.
- ***A Mind for Numbers* (Barbara Oakley)** — the evergreen of learning science (focused vs. diffuse mode, memory chunking), with steady traffic to [review sites](https://skyreaderpapa.com/a-mind-for-numbers/) and [long-form walkthroughs](https://maxjamesread.com/a-mind-for-numbers/) (in Chinese), now read as the "how to learn fast and well" playbook for the AI era.

## Psychology / Inner Growth

- ***Counselling for Toads* (Robert de Board)** — the hottest book in this category for 2026. Manager Today's Threads post on the "Poor Little Old Me" mind game (November 2025) drew [84.2K views and 2.3K likes](https://www.threads.com/@managertoday_official/post/DRrgnSJgM2c/), with comment sections full of "this is literally my mom" recognition; derivative threads on "psychological games / victim scripts" were still burning in June 2026. [Vocus](https://vocus.cc/article/68ea79c8fd897800015739c9) and [Dcard](https://www.dcard.tw/f/book/p/239024315) (in Chinese) keep producing write-ups.
- ***The Courage to Be Disliked* (Ichiro Kishimi & Fumitake Koga)** — ten years in print and still the champion of psychology primers; "separation of tasks" is now shared community vocabulary. [Vocus published fresh essays in late 2025](https://vocus.cc/article/68c7815dfd89780001396988) (in Chinese); the long-running crossfire between devotees and "it dismisses trauma" critics keeps it from cooling off.
- ***Maybe You Should Talk to Someone* (Lori Gottlieb)** — the therapist-who-sees-a-therapist double perspective, a fixture of psychology reading lists, with [new Vocus reviews in mid-2025](https://vocus.cc/article/685a884efd897800016bd740) (in Chinese) and a 2024 sequel extending its run.
- ***Inner Force* (Eric Wu)** — the leading homegrown Taiwanese title. The author keeps the flywheel spinning with new books, an active Facebook community, and lecture circuits — an "Inner Force universe" — and [Reading Outpost's walkthrough](https://readingoutpost.com/inner-force/) (in Chinese) remains a search staple.

## Money / Wealth Mindset

- ***The Psychology of Money* (Morgan Housel)** — a near-mandatory entry on every Threads finance book list in June 2026: the "[if I could only recommend 4 finance books](https://www.threads.com/@yuna__corgi/post/DZsUmuemcOO)" post (11.8K views) and multiple "must-read" lists put it first. [Reading Outpost's long-form guide](https://readingoutpost.com/psychology-of-money/) (in Chinese) keeps getting cited.
- ***Just Keep Buying* (Nick Maggiulli)** — dubbed "the *Atomic Habits* of investing." The author's 2026 release *The Wealth Ladder* ran hot on Threads and fed attention back to this earlier book; a [December 2025 Vocus review](https://vocus.cc/article/6936ef32fd8978000137dcee) (in Chinese) frames it as "a prescription for long-term investing anxiety," and testimonies of switching from dividend stocks to index funds are common.
- ***Die with Zero* (Bill Perkins)** — the "memory dividend" philosophy of spending keeps compounding: [Dcard write-ups](https://www.dcard.tw/f/book/p/258561675) and [PTT book board threads](https://www.ptt.cc/bbs/book/M.1694705678.A.555.html) (in Chinese), frequently staged against *Just Keep Buying*'s discipline camp.
- ***Rich Dad Poor Dad* (Robert Kiyosaki)** — the byword for financial-literacy awakening, kept alive by its own crossfire: [a Threads reflection on reading it at 35 ("pay yourself first")](https://www.threads.com/@wukuangyu/post/DaAD08dE8bJ/) and [a Dcard piece extending it into real investing practice](https://www.dcard.tw/@hearth1236/post/260802172) (in Chinese), while "this book has no substance and still tops Taiwan's charts" rant videos fuel the other side. Both camps extend its life.
- ***The Millionaire Next Door* (Thomas Stanley)** — the empirical study of 500+ millionaires. A [Threads finance book list](https://www.threads.com/@houyingli/post/DPIIFa4jW92/) devotes a section to its "self-made wealth is frugal and lives below its means" finding, resonating with Taiwan's "flexing vs. pragmatism" mood.

A borderline case worth watching: ***The Almanack of Naval Ravikant*** frequently tops 2026 lists and sits between money and mindset; if the list ever expands from "personal finance" to "wealth thinking," it is the strongest candidate.

## Three Reusable Patterns

More valuable than the list itself are three structural patterns that emerged from the research:

1. **Threads is the main arena for book discussion in 2026.** The algorithm chains same-topic posts into related threads, repeatedly reigniting evergreen titles — *Rewire* burning from a single August 2025 post through July 2026 is the textbook case. Dcard and PTT supply the long tail of write-ups; Vocus supplies depth. The three layers complement each other.
2. **Waki Chen (Reading Outpost) is the single biggest node for self-growth books in Chinese.** The discussion lines for *How Will You Measure Your Life?*, *Hidden Potential*, *Company of One*, and *The Psychology of Money* all run through him, and his own *Work Without a Job* became the "read before you resign" totem. Tracking one KOL's output predicts the next wave of book lists.
3. **"New book as inlet, classic as destination" is how evergreen books get their lives extended.** *Rewire*'s breakout sent readers back to *The Brain That Changes Itself*; *The Wealth Ladder*'s heat fed *Just Keep Buying*. When scouting evergreen titles, "does this topic have a new gateway book this year?" beats reading bestseller charts.
