A lot of people ask me: why do you feel comfortable holding 2x leveraged ETF (正2) for the long term?

My answer isn't "I'm very confident." It's "my system doesn't require confidence."

This system isn't something I invented. I assembled it from three books.

---

## [*A Random Walk Down Wall Street*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Random_Walk_Down_Wall_Street) — Hold the Index

Burton Malkiel's core argument: markets are efficient, and most active managers can't beat the index over the long run. Instead of picking stocks, buy a fund that represents the entire market and let compounding do its work.

This book made me stop picking stocks and switch to index ETFs. It answered the question: *what* to hold.

---

## [*The Leveraged ETF Investing Method*](https://www.books.com.tw/products/0010953374) — Amplify Returns

Lin Cheng-hua's book analyzes the long-term behavior of 2x leveraged ETFs — specifically 00631L (元大台灣50正2): in trending bull markets, 2x products deliver significantly higher returns than their unleveraged counterparts, at the cost of greater volatility and deeper drawdowns.

The key insight: the longer the holding period, and with regular rebalancing, the less significant the volatility drag becomes. That gave me the confidence to use 2x leverage within a long-term framework.

---

## [*The Psychology of Money*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Psychology_of_Money) — Leave Room for Error

The most important thing Morgan Housel says in *The Psychology of Money*: smart planning isn't about being more precise — it's about deliberately leaving room for error.

In practice, this means: don't go all-in on leverage. Hold low-volatility assets (e.g. bond ETFs or cash) alongside 2x ETFs to cushion extreme scenarios. This book established my "2x ETF + low-volatility asset" portfolio blend rather than betting everything on leverage.

---

## The Execution Mechanism: Rebalancing

The three books gave me the philosophy. Rebalancing gives me the execution.

Resetting portfolio weights to the target allocation every quarter automatically achieves:
- After a big run-up: trim 2x exposure (sell some near the highs)
- After a big drop: add to 2x exposure (buy more near the lows)

No need to call the top or bottom. The mechanism itself does the "buy low, sell high."

## The System

| Question | Answer | Source |
|---|---|---|
| What to hold? | Index ETF (2x leveraged) | *A Random Walk Down Wall Street* |
| How to accelerate? | Moderate leverage | *The Leveraged ETF Investing Method* |

I don't predict the market, don't guess tops and bottoms, and don't chase hot stocks. I just designed a system that can keep running no matter which way the market moves — then let time do the rest.
